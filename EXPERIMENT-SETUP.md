## How to Record Eclipse Shadow Bands (For Eclipse on August 12, 2026)

### What You Need
- A **smartphone** (with video recording)
- A **camera stand or tripod**
- A **white sheet or white poster board**
- Tape, clips, or weights (to secure the sheet)

---

### Step 1: Pick the Right Spot
- See [Path of Eclipse on August 12, 2026](https://nso.edu/for-public/eclipse-map-2026/)
- Find a location with a clear view toward the **northwest horizon**
- A **wall or side of a building** works well for mounting the sheet
- Make sure nothing blocks the low Sun
---
### Step 2: Set Up the White Sheet (Vertical)
- Attach the **white sheet vertically** to a wall or flat surface  
- Try to keep it:
  - **Flat and smooth** (minimize wrinkles)
  - **Tightly secured** so it doesn’t move in the wind
- The sheet should be facing roughly toward the **northwest horizon**
- A vertical sheet works especially well when the Sun is low, because the light hits it more directly
---
### Step 3: Position the Camera
- Place your phone on a **tripod** facing the sheet
- The camera should point **straight at the sheet** (not at an angle)
- Fill most of the frame with the white surface
- Keep the setup **steady and fixed**
---
### Step 4: Align with the Sun
- Make sure the sheet is **facing the Sun’s direction** (northwest, low in the sky)
- If possible, keep the sheet roughly **perpendicular to the incoming sunlight**
- This improves contrast and makes shadow bands easier to see
---
### Step 5: Camera Settings
- Use **video mode**
- Record at **60 fps** if available
- Turn off or lock:
  - Auto-focus  
  - Auto-exposure  
- Keep brightness consistent
- Avoid making any adjustments to camera settings once recording starts
---
### Step 6: When to Start Recording
- Record a 5-minute sample of video 1-hour before totality. This provides a control data set and baseline.
- Start recording **10 minutes before totality**
- Continue **through totality and 10 minutes after**
- Shadow bands usually appear a few minutes before and after totality
---
### Step 7: What to Look For
- Watch for **faint, shimmering light and dark bands**
- They may look like:
  - Ripples  
  - Waves  
  - Heat shimmer patterns  
- They can move quickly across the sheet
---
### Step 8: Stay Hands-Off
- Do **not touch the camera or sheet**
- Keep everything stable during recording
---
### Optional Tips
- If possible, use a **second camera** from a different angle
- Record **audio notes** (time, what you observe)
- Note your **location and time**

---

## Safety Reminder
- **Do not look directly at the Sun** without proper eclipse glasses
- You can record shadow bands without looking at the Sun

---
